1. 2030 Convertible Senior Notes ($125.0 million / 2.750% / due 2030)

1.1 Origin & principal outstanding

  • The 2030 Notes were created on December 23, 2024 when AAOI ran a privately-negotiated exchange: it swapped roughly 76.7million * *★(FY202510 − K, NoteL)ofitsold2026Notesfor(i) * *125.0 million ★ (= one hundred twenty-five million dollars; 0.125billion)aggregateprincipalofnew2.75089.6 thousand** ★ of cash (accrued interest + fractional shares).   • Principal outstanding (2030 Notes): $125.0 million ★ at Dec 31, 2025 and at Mar 31, 2026 (FY2025 10-K Note L; Q1-2026 10-Q Note 9).
  • 2026 Notes stub: Only $3.5 million ★ principal of the old 5.250% 2026 Notes survived the December 2024 Exchanges. On July 30, 2025 AAOI retired that final $3.5 million by issuing 239,404 shares ★ of common stock plus cash for accrued interest. 2026 Notes principal outstanding = $0 ★ at Dec 31, 2025 (FY2025 10-K Note L). The remaining convertible debt is entirely the 2030 Notes.

1.2 Coupon, maturity, conversion terms

  • Coupon: 2.750% ★ per year, paid semi-annually in arrears on January 15 and July 15, first payment July 15, 2025 (FY2025 10-K Note L).
  • Maturity: January 15, 2030 ★, unless earlier converted, redeemed or repurchased.
  • Indenture / trustee: Indenture dated December 23, 2024 between AAOI (issuer) and Computershare Trust Company, N.A. (trustee) ★.
  • Conversion rate: 23.0884 shares of common stock per $1,000 principal ★ — equivalent to a conversion price of approximately $43.31 per share ★.
  • The conversion price was struck at a 27.50% premium ★ over the $33.97 last reported sale price on December 18, 2024.
  • Settlement method: AAOI may settle conversions in cash, shares, or a combination, at the Company's election ★ (cash/share flexibility — important for dilution control).

1.3 Holder conversion conditions

  • Before October 15, 2029: holders may convert only upon satisfaction of a common-stock sale-price condition or a note-trading-price condition (as defined in the Indenture), or upon certain events including a Fundamental Change, Make-Whole Fundamental Change, or Common Stock Change Event ★ (FY2025 10-K Note L).
  • From and after October 15, 2029: holders may convert at any time until the close of business on the second scheduled trading day before maturity ★.

1.4 Issuer redemption right (standard call)

  • AAOI may redeem the 2030 Notes, in whole or in part, on or after January 15, 2027 and on/before the 40th scheduled trading day before maturity ★ (FY2025 10-K Note L).
  • Redemption price: principal + accrued and unpaid interest (cash) ★.
  • Stock-price trigger: redemption is allowed only if the last reported sale price exceeds 130% of the conversion price (i.e., ~$56.30) on (1) at least 20 trading days (not necessarily consecutive) during the 30 consecutive trading days ending on the trading day before the redemption notice, and (2) the trading day immediately before the notice ★.
  • Calling a note for redemption constitutes a Make-Whole Fundamental Change for that note, which can increase the conversion rate if it is converted after being called ★.

1.5 "Specified Divestiture" special redemption / repurchase right

  • The Indenture has an unusual feature: if a "Specified Divestiture" (defined in the Indenture) is completed, AAOI may separately redeem the notes at par + accrued interest (no stock-price trigger), OR — unless AAOI has elected to redeem all of them — each holder may require AAOI to repurchase its notes for cash on a date 20–35 business days after AAOI's divestiture notice, at principal + accrued interest ★ (FY2025 10-K Note L). This is a deal-contingent put.

1.6 Fundamental-change repurchase right

  • On a fundamental change (as defined), holders may require AAOI to repurchase all or part of their notes for cash at 100% of principal plus accrued and unpaid interest ★ (FY2025 10-K Note L).

1.7 Capped call / bond hedge — NO

  • There is NO capped call and NO bond hedge / convertible note hedge disclosed. Search of the FY2025 10-K returned zero hits for "capped call" and the dilution-mitigation discussion is absent. The only dilution mitigant is AAOI's flexible cash/share/combination settlement election (§1.2). → Capped call / bond hedge: not present (not disclosed in filing).

1.8 Covenants — 2030 Notes

  • The 2030 Notes are covenant-light: "The 2030 Notes do not restrict the Company's ability to incur debt or liens. No sinking fund is provided... There are no guarantors of the 2030 Notes." ★ (FY2025 10-K Note L). Subject only to customary events of default.
  • No financial maintenance covenants (no leverage or liquidity ratio) on the notes themselves.

2. 2023 PSU Cash-Settlement Risk (~$65 million)

2.1 The mechanism

  • AAOI grants senior executives performance stock units (PSUs) under the legacy 2021 Plan, vesting over three years; payout ranges 0% to 200% of target ★ (FY2025 10-K share-based comp note; DEF 14A).
  • The risk: AAOI's existing equity-plan share pool can run dry when PSUs vest at the maximum 200% level. Cash settlement is expressly permitted by the terms of the applicable PSU agreements ★ (DEF 14A, 2026 Plan proposal). When the pool is short, AAOI settles in shares to the extent of available capacity and settles the remainder in cash.
  • Precedent already happened: for the 2021–2024 PSUs earned at 200%, AAOI had "insufficient shares remaining in the pool under the 2021 Plan" and "settled the remainder in cash" ★ (2025 DEF 14A — tm252340). NEO 2021–2024 PSUs earned at 200% included CEO Thompson Lin 252,844 shares, CFO Stefan Murry 88,495, etc. This confirms cash settlement is a live, recurring practice — not hypothetical.

2.2 The 2023 PSUs and the ~$65 million figure

  • The DEF 14A (filed 2026-04-24) discloses, in connection with Proposal to approve the 2026 Equity Incentive Plan ("2026 Plan"), a table of PSUs granted in 2023 that may need to be fully or partially settled in cash, instead of shares, if the maximum (200%) level of performance is achieved AND the 2026 Plan is not approved ★.
  • Per-executive exposure (★, all DEF 14A, dollar values computed at the $84.59 Mar 31, 2026 closing price ★):
    • Thompson Lin (CEO): $30,103,128 / 355,871 PSUs
    • Stefan Murry (CFO): $10,536,107 / 124,555 PSUs
    • Hung-Lun (Fred) Chang (SVP, NA GM): $9,482,454 / 112,099 PSUs
    • Shu-Hua (Joshua) Yeh (SVP, Asia GM): $8,127,830 / 96,085 PSUs

    • All current executive officers as a group (5 persons): $64,872,156 / 766,901 PSUs** ★ — this is the **"~$65 million" ($0.065 billion) figure.
  • Directors and non-executive employees: $0 / 0 ★ (the cash-settlement exposure is concentrated entirely in the 5 named executives).

2.3 Dependency on the 2026 Plan shareholder vote

  • The cash-settlement obligation is contingent on the 2026 Plan NOT being approved by shareholders ★. If shareholders approve the 2026 Plan, the new share reserve becomes available and the 2023 PSUs can be settled in stock instead of cash, avoiding the ~$65M cash drain.
  • 2026 Plan share economics (DEF 14A, as of Mar 31, 2026): 2,500,000 ★ new shares authorized; 418,418 ★ remaining under Prior Plans; 2,918,418 ★ total available for future awards if approved. Outstanding PSUs (at target) = 1,359,359 ★; outstanding RSUs = 1,479,352 ★.
  • Up to 2,838,711 ★ shares subject to outstanding Prior-Plan awards may roll into the 2026 Plan reserve (capped so Prior-Plan additions won't exceed 5,757,129 ★ shares).
  • Dilution: approving the 2026 Plan raises total potential dilution from 5.01% to 8.18% ★ of shares outstanding. Shares outstanding = 78,943,144 ★ at Mar 31, 2026.

Key takeaways

  1. 2030 Notes = $125.0M, 2.750%, due Jan 15 2030, conversion 43.31(23.0884sh/1,000). Covenant-light (no debt/lien restriction, no sinking fund, no guarantors). No capped call / bond hedge. Issuer call from Jan 15 2027 only above 130% of conversion price (20-of-30-day test); plus a deal-contingent "Specified Divestiture" redemption/put and a 100%-par fundamental-change put.
  2. Fair value exploded from $145.2M (Dec 2025) to $278.7M (Mar 2026) as the stock ran to $84.59 — the notes are now deep ITM; conversion/dilution, not cash repayment, is the base case.
  3. Bank covenants (incl. BOK Financial $35M 3-yr revolver, accordion to $75M) restrict additional debt, liens, asset sales and M&A, but no numeric leverage/liquidity threshold is disclosed.
  4. 2023 PSU cash-settlement risk = up to $64,872,156 / 766,901 PSUs across 5 executives at 200% max performance, triggered only if shareholders fail to approve the 2026 Equity Incentive Plan. Cash settlement is expressly allowed by the PSU agreements and AAOI has already done it for the 2021–2024 PSUs.
