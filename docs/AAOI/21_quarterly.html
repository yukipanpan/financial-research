4. Narrative — Inflection, Margin & Burn

Revenue inflection: 2025Q1. ★◆ Revenue oscillated in a $40–65M band through 2023–2024 (no durable growth — 2024 quarters were actually below the comparable 2023 quarters early in the year). The clean break is 2025Q1, when revenue jumped to $99.9M (+53% QoQ from 2024Q3's 65.2M) * *andhasclimbedeveryquartersinceto * *151.1M in 2026Q1 — roughly 3.7× the 2024Q1 trough. ◆

CATV overtakes Data Center: 2025Q1. ★ The mix flips hard at exactly the revenue inflection. CATV had collapsed to 13–32% of sales through 2024 as a North American MSO (cable operator) network-upgrade cycle paused; Data Center was 63–79%. In 2025Q1 CATV snapped to 64.6% ($64.5M) and stayed the #1 market through 2025Q3 (~55–60%), funneled mainly through Digicomm. By **2026Q1 Data Center re-took the lead (53.9%, 81.4M) * *asAI/hyperscaleropticsramped—butCATVstayedhugeat44.266.8M). So the company now rides two large engines instead of one. ◆

Gross margin: structurally re-rated, then stable. ★◆ GM ran 17–24% in 2023–2024 (with a one-off spike to 32.3% in 2023Q3). With the volume inflection, GM stepped up to a 28–31% band from 2025Q1 onward (30.6% → 30.3% → 28.0% → 29.1%). The improvement is volume/absorption- and mix-driven; margin has plateaued in the high-20s/low-30s rather than continuing to climb.

Operating loss: narrowing but not yet gone. ★◆ The loss is not a clean glide-path. It bottomed at –26.2Min2024Q2 * *(peakburnquarter, GMonly228.9M in 2025Q1 on the revenue jump. It has since widened modestly again (–16.0M, –18.2M, –$13.0M) as opex scaled: R&D tripled from $8.5M (2023Q1) to $25.7M (2026Q1)** and SG&A roughly doubled. Net loss per share has compressed dramatically — from –$0.56/–0.66to * *–0.19 — but that is as much a function of the ballooning share count as of operating improvement. The company remains GAAP-unprofitable across all 10 quarters.

Cash & balance sheet: burn replaced by heavy capital raising. ★ Cash fell to a danger-zone $8.4M (2024Q1), then was repeatedly rebuilt via external financing rather than operations: the $125M 2030 convertible (Dec-2024), the Amazon warrant deal, and large ATM/equity issuance. Cash rose $16M → $34M → $51M → 65M → **137M (2025Q3)** → **439.7M(2026Q1) * *.The2026Q1cashspikereflectsamajorcapitalraise, notoperatingcashgeneration. * *Workingcapitalisexplodingalongsiderevenue : * * inventorygrewfrom 54M (2024) to **206M(2026Q1) * *, andARfrom 57M to $299M (2026Q1) — with Digicomm alone owing $222.7M (74.5% of AR) on extended terms, the single biggest balance-sheet risk.

Share count: relentless dilution. ★◆ Weighted-avg diluted shares went from 28.9M (2023Q1) to 76.0M (2026Q1) — ~2.6× in nine quarters (~+11% per quarter on average), via ATM equity, the S-8 plans, converts and the Amazon warrant overhang (another ~7.9M potential shares). Per-share losses are shrinking partly because the denominator keeps growing.

Bottom line: AAOI inflected in 2025Q1 on a CATV (Digicomm-led) surge layered onto a recovering AI data-center business; gross margin re-rated to ~30% and stuck there; the operating loss narrowed off the 2024Q2 bottom but has not closed and opex is scaling fast; the cash position looks strong only because of aggressive equity/convert issuance and is shadowed by a $299M receivable book concentrated 74.5% in one customer and a ~2.6× expansion in the share count.
