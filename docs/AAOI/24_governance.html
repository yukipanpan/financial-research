E) SEC comment letter — 2023-01-11 "UPLOAD" + 2023-03-17 "CORRESP"

Both fetched and read. (The UPLOAD is a scanned PDF; rendered to image and read directly — plain-text extraction was garbled.)

What the SEC actually said — and the key finding

  • ★ The 2023-01-11 UPLOAD is NOT a substantive comment letter. It is a one-paragraph "no-review" notice from the SEC Division of Corporation Finance (Office of Manufacturing), addressed to GC David Kuo, regarding AAOI's Form S-3 registration statement (File No. 333-269132, filed Jan 5, 2023). Verbatim: "This is to advise you that we have not reviewed and will not review your registration statement... Please refer to Rules 460 and 461 regarding requests for acceleration. We remind you that the company and its management are responsible for the accuracy and adequacy of their disclosures..." Signed Erin Donahue.
  • ★ The 2023-03-17 CORRESP is the company's matching request to accelerate effectiveness of that same S-3 (to 10:00am CDT, Mar 21, 2023), with the standard Rule 461 acknowledgments (responsibility for disclosure adequacy, etc.), signed by GC David Kuo, counsel Haynes and Boone.

Resolution / what it reveals

  • There was NO SEC scrutiny of AAOI's accounting or disclosure here. The SEC explicitly declined to review the shelf registration; the company then requested and obtained acceleration so the S-3 could go effective. This pair documents a routine shelf-registration effectiveness mechanic, not an accounting/disclosure investigation.
  • Important caveat for the research file: Despite the index labels, this correspondence does not reveal any substantive SEC concern. If the project needs evidence of genuine SEC accounting scrutiny of AAOI (e.g., revenue recognition, customer concentration, inventory/impairment), it would have to come from a different comment-letter cycle — none is present in this 2023-01/2023-03 exchange.
