3. Customer / supply agreements

Amazon — up to $4 billion warrant (the headline deal)

  • ★ 2025-03-13 (Item 1.01 / 3.02): Transaction Agreement with Amazon.com, Inc. A warrant issued to an Amazon subsidiary (Amazon.com NV Investment Holdings LLC) to purchase up to 7,945,399 shares at $23.6954/share.
    • 1,324,233 shares vested immediately on issuance.
    • The remaining shares vest over time based on Amazon's discretionary purchases, up to a cumulative $4 billion of purchases.
    • Warrant exercisable until March 13, 2035; customary registration rights.
  • ★ 2025-04-25 (Item 8.01): AAOI filed a resale prospectus covering all 7,945,399 warrant shares for Amazon as selling stockholder.
  • This effectively ties Amazon (AWS) to AAOI as a major customer with an equity incentive scaled to a $4B purchase commitment — a transformational anchor-customer relationship.

Microsoft — laser/transceiver supply (earlier relationship)

  • ★ SOW No.1 – Foundry (effective 12/16/2022; disclosed 2023-05-03): AAOI to manufacture lasers to Microsoft spec; milestones through 12/30/2025. Microsoft holds a right of first refusal to acquire the Company in a change-of-control. Dollar size redacted.
  • ★ SOW No.2 – Design & Assembly (2023-06-21): design + assembly services; 5-year initial term, auto-renewal, dedicated production line, 3-year warranty. Dollar size redacted.

Other supply/build agreements (capacity expansion)

  • ★ 2024-12-31: Chyi Ding Technologies cleanroom construction, NTD 277M (Taiwan Linkou).
  • ★ 2026-03-05: LCC3 Solution Inc. FAB2 cleanroom design-build, ~$30.885M (Sugar Land TX); liquidated-damages cap 1.6M, milestone/performanceincentivesupto 5.1M combined.

5. Notable / red flags

  1. ★ Earnings guidance correction (8-K/A, 2024-02-23): AAOI re-filed its Q4'23 release to correct Q1'24 outlook — non-GAAP net loss revised from (18.9)–(20.8)M to (10.9)–(12.6)M. A material restatement of furnished guidance within a day of the original.
  2. ★ Disclosed material weakness: the 2026-05-08 auditor-change 8-K confirms a material weakness in controls over review of technical accounting analysis, first disclosed in the FY2024 10-K, remediated as of 12/31/2025.
  3. ★ Auditor change (2026-05-08): Grant Thornton dismissed after a competitive process; PwC appointed for FY2026. Filing states no disagreements and no reportable events other than the (remediated) material weakness. The upgrade to a Big Four auditor alongside the Amazon deal and a $600M ATM is consistent with scaling/credibility but the timing of an auditor switch always warrants attention.
  4. ★ Aggressive serial equity dilution: ATM capacity escalated $35M → 600Min 3years, withatleast * *68.6M (2023) + $250.0M (by Mar-2026)** confirmed sold, plus a $33.7M registered direct and ~2.0M+ exchange shares — heavy reliance on equity to fund the capacity build.
  5. ★ Convertible-note covenant stripping (twice): both the 2023 (2024→2026 notes) and 2024 (2026→2030 notes) exchanges included supplemental indentures that eliminated restrictive covenants on additional debt/liens for the remaining stub notes — standard in exchanges but reduces protection for non-participating holders.
  6. ★ Microsoft change-of-control ROFR: Microsoft's SOW No.1 gives it a right of first refusal to acquire AAOI on a change of control — a structural constraint on any future takeover (and a potential conflict with Amazon's new equity stake).
  7. ★ Massive real-estate commitment ramp (2025-2026): within ~8 months AAOI signed/added: Sugar Land lease (~210K sf), Houston Blue Ridge lease + 30.26Mpurchaseoption, Pearland * *58.43M outright purchase**, three Hightower Houston buildings with **102.25M * *aggregatepurchaseoption, plusa 30.9M FAB2 cleanroom build — a very large, mostly-US capacity expansion being financed largely by equity.
  8. ★ China credit churn: five China/Taiwan bank facilities were opened/refinanced/terminated in rapid succession in 2024-2025 (CZB, SPD, CCB, Taishin), suggesting active short-term working-capital management and a shift of bank relationships.
