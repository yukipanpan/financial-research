2. Customer Warrant (Amazon) — FY2025 only (★ FY2025 10-K, Note C / Revenue)

  • ★ Issued March 13, 2025 to a wholly-owned subsidiary of Amazon.com, Inc.
  • ★ Warrant to purchase up to 7,945,399 shares at exercise price $23.6956/share; 10-yr contractual term.
  • ★ 1,324,233 shares vested/exercisable at issuance; remaining 6,621,166 shares vest over 10 yr contingent on Amazon aggregate purchases of $4 billion of AAOI products.
  • Treatment: ★ Equity-classified share-based consideration payable to a customer → recognized as a reduction of revenue (contra-revenue) from Amazon as goods/services transfer. Not a liability, not a derivative.
  • Measurement: ★ Grant-date fair value 12.64/share * *via * *Black − Scholes − Merton * *.Inputsatgrant : expectedvolatility * *80.0015.87.
  • Balance-sheet recognition (vested tranche): ★ As of 12/31/2025, recorded other current asset $1.0M + other non-current asset 15.0M * * = **16.0M aggregate grant-date FV of the 1,324,233 vested Warrant Shares (a prepaid/asset, amortized against revenue).
  • P&L impact FY2025: ★ "Warrants contra revenue" addback in cash flow = $0.823M (★ Note, cash flow statement). Filing states the arrangements' impact "was not material for the periods presented."
  • ◆ Note: ★ "recognized approximately $33.1 million of revenue associated with customer arrangements that included the issuance of warrants" — i.e. Amazon-related revenue in FY2025 ≈ 33.1M(gross), againstwhichonly 0.8M contra-revenue was netted in-year (asset amortizes as the $4B purchase target is approached).

7. Risk Factors (Item 1A) — Top / Most Material (★ FY2025 10-K)

The FY2025 risk taxonomy reordered to put a new lead risk first:

  1. ★ AI-demand durability / U.S. CapEx returns (NEW in FY2025, listed first): "Significant capital investments in U.S. manufacturing and automation... intended to support AI-related demand, may not achieve expected returns." If AI demand "does not materialize, is delayed, or develops differently," AAOI faces overcapacity, low utilization, negative operating leverage, stranded assets, and long-lived-asset/CIP impairment. Take-or-pay/minimum purchase commitments may exceed needs.
  2. ★ Customer concentration: "a limited number of key customers account for a significant portion of our revenue" (Digicomm 53% / Microsoft 29% in FY25).
  3. ★ Tariffs / trade policy: "changes in U.S. tariff and import/export regulations" + dedicated section "Risks Related to International Trade and Operations" — U.S./international trade policy changes; export & import controls.
  4. ★ China operations: dedicated section — economic/political policy changes in China; high direct-labor turnover; regulation of loans to the China subsidiary; rising China labor costs. (Material given China-made revenue = $262M / 57% in FY25.)
  5. ★ Indebtedness & covenants: "restrictive covenants in loan agreements"; "indebtedness and liabilities could limit cash flow"; ability to obtain capital "on favorable terms or at all."
  6. ★ Demand forecasting & high fixed costs: difficulty matching production to demand; "high fixed costs due to vertical integration."
  7. ★ Liquidity / dilution: ability to maintain sufficient liquidity (forward-looking statements); heavy ATM reliance (FY25 +$518.9M equity).
  8. ★ NOL limitations, FX (USD/RMB/NT$), product qualification, key personnel, single-source suppliers.

No going-concern risk factor in any of the three years. Management states existing cash + operating cash flow + credit are "sufficient... for the next 12 months" (FY2025). No Digicomm-specific risk factor beyond the generic customer-concentration risk.
