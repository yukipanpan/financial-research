A. Data-Center Product Roadmap (800G / 1.6T)

A.1 What the filings actually disclose

AAOI does not publish unit volumes or per-product revenue by speed grade. The roadmap detail below is the entirety of what the FY2025 10-K and Q1-2026 10-Q reveal — everything more granular (per-customer SKU mix, qualification dates, exact ship dates) is Not disclosed / confidential.

  • ★ Speed-grade roadmap guidance (the single most concrete statement): "We expect continued sales of our 40 Gbps, 100 Gbps, and 400 Gbps products in 2026, and we expect that sales of 800 Gbps products will likely exceed sales of 400 Gbps products later in 2026. However, quarter-to-quarter results may show considerable variability as is usual in a period of technology transition." — FY2025 10-K, 2026-02-26.
    • ◆ Read-through: 400G is the current volume workhorse; 800G is ramping and projected to overtake 400G in 2H-2026. 1.6T is not yet a revenue product.
  • ★ R&D is funding "100 Gbps, 200/400/800/1,600 Gbps data center products" plus DOCSIS 4.0 / 1.8 GHz CATV. "During 2025, we experienced increased interest in new technologies like 800G and 1.6T among our data center customers. Supporting customer testing and qualification for these new opportunities materially increased our R&D expenditures." — FY2025 10-K, 2026-02-26. (R&D rose **30.6million, +55.611.7M / +44.4%-ish QoQ trend continues into Q1-26 with R&D of $25.7M.) ★
    • ◆ So 1.6T is explicitly still in the customer-testing / qualification stage as of FY2025 — i.e. design-in, not volume shipment. 800G is described as both shipping ("our 800G products in particular are used to interconnect AI compute infrastructure") and still attracting new qualification interest — consistent with an early-ramp product.
  • ★ 800G = the AI product. "Our products are used to interconnect network elements within data centers generally, and our 800G products in particular are used to interconnect AI compute infrastructure." — FY2025 10-K, 2026-02-26. The AI thesis rests on 800G+ (and the future 1.6T).
  • ★ Market framing: optical transport "typically providing speeds from 10 Gbps to 1.6 Tbps"; AI "driving the need for faster (800Gbps and higher)" optics — FY2025 10-K & Q1-2026 10-Q. Industry tailwind language only; not a shipment claim.

A.2 Design wins (a proxy for product traction)

  • ★ "In 2025, 2024 and 2023, we had 9, 8 and 6 design wins, respectively." — FY2025 10-K, 2026-02-26. A design win = customer has tested and committed to purchase. AAOI cautions design-win count does not map cleanly to revenue ("some design wins result in significant revenue and some do not").
    • ◆ Modest absolute growth (6 → 8 → 9); the revenue surge is driven more by volume/scale on existing wins than by a wave of new wins.

A.3 Which products tie to Microsoft vs Amazon

  • ★ Microsoft is the named data-center customer, not Amazon. The two Microsoft Statements of Work (SOW No.1 Foundry, eff. 12/16/2022; SOW No.2 Design & Assembly, eff. 6/21/2023; 8-Ks 2023-05-03 / 2023-06-21) cover laser manufacturing + design/assembly — i.e. AAOI builds lasers/transceivers to Microsoft spec. Dollar size redacted in the 8-K bodies. Microsoft = 28.8% of FY2025 revenue (was 43.7%/46.6% in 2024, 18.4%/46.6% in 2023 — the 10-K gives slightly different 2023/2024 figures in different sections). ★ FY2025 10-K.
  • ★ Amazon's product tie is NOT specified by speed grade anywhere in the filings. The Amazon relationship is documented only through the Customer Warrant (see §B) — "purchases by or on behalf of Amazon and its affiliates ... of the Company's products." The filings never state whether Amazon is buying 400G, 800G, or 1.6T. Not disclosed. Given the AI framing and that 800G is "used to interconnect AI compute infrastructure," 800G is the likely Amazon/AWS product, but the filing does not confirm it.
  • ◆ Net: the disclosed product-to-customer map is thin. Microsoft = lasers/transceivers (SOW-defined, redacted $). Amazon = unspecified "products" tied to a $4B warrant. The bulk of the 2025 revenue surge actually came from CATV via Digicomm, a distributor — not a hyperscaler product line.

B. Amazon Customer Warrant — Vesting Progress ("Is Amazon buying yet?")

B.1 The instrument

  • ★ Issued March 13, 2025 to Amazon.com NV Investment Holdings LLC (an Amazon subsidiary). Warrant for up to 7,945,399 shares, exercise price $23.6956, 10-year term (expires 3/13/2035). — FY2025 10-K & Q1-2026 10-Q.
  • ★ 1,324,233 shares vested immediately at issuance. The remaining 6,621,166 shares "may vest over the next 10 years, dependent on aggregate purchases by or on behalf of Amazon and its affiliates of $4 billion (four billion dollars) of the Company's products over this time period." — FY2025 10-K, 2026-02-26 & Q1-2026 10-Q, 2026-05-07.
  • ★ Grant-date fair value $12.64/share (Black-Scholes-Merton; 80% vol, 10-yr term, 4.23% risk-free, $15.87 share price at grant). — FY2025 10-K.

B.2 How much has vested beyond the initial tranche? → ★ ZERO additional shares

This is the load-bearing finding, and it is negative:

  • ★ As of December 31, 2025, AAOI carried the warrant as an asset of $1.0 million (other current) + 15.0million(othernoncurrent)= 16.0 million, explicitly "representing the aggregate grant-date fair value of 1,324,233 Warrant Shares vested at the issuance." — FY2025 10-K. → still only the initial tranche vested.
  • ★ As of March 31, 2026, the corresponding assets are $3.9 million (other current) + 12.6million(othernoncurrent)= 16.5 million — still tied to the original 1,324,233 shares. — Q1-2026 10-Q.
  • ★ The Q1-2026 dilution table confirms it: the "Amazon warrant" line counts only 1,324 (thousand) = 1,324,233 shares as in-the-money/dilutive — exactly the initial tranche, no incremental vesting. — Q1-2026 10-Q EPS footnote.
  • ◆ Conclusion: through Q1-2026, none of the performance-vesting 6,621,166 shares have vested. Amazon has not yet bought enough to trigger any milestone. The $4 billion ramp is, on the evidence of the filings, barely started.

B.3 Is Amazon actually buying — and the $33.1M / $38.0M figure

  • ★ "For the year ended December 31, 2025, the Company recognized approximately $33.1 million** of revenue associated with customer arrangements that included the issuance of warrants." (FY2025 10-K) and "For the three months ended March 31, 2026 ... approximately **$38.0 million of revenue associated with customer arrangements that included the issuance of warrants." (Q1-2026 10-Q).
    • ◆ Interpretation (with a caveat — the phrasing is deliberately opaque): these are the gross revenue dollars from the warrant-linked (Amazon) arrangement against which the warrant fair value is amortized as a reduction of revenue (contra-revenue). The accounting is: recognize grant-date FV "as a reduction of revenue from Amazon as the related goods or services are transferred." So yes, Amazon is buying — roughly $33.1 million (FY2025, ~9.5 months) and $38.0 million (Q1-2026 alone) of gross product. The QoQ acceleration (Q1-26 single quarter > full FY25) signals Amazon purchases ramped sharply into early 2026.
  • ★ BUT Amazon is not large enough to appear in the customer-concentration table: in FY2025 only Digicomm (53.1%) and Microsoft (28.8%) exceed 10%; in 2024, Microsoft / Digicomm / Oracle. Amazon is never listed as a >10% customer → its ~$33M (FY25) is ~7% of $455.7M revenue. ★
  • ◆ Reconciling against the 4Btarget : * * atacumulative 33M (2025) + ~38M(Q1 − 26) ≈ ** 71 million of Amazon purchases to date, AAOI is roughly **1.8% of the way to the 4.0billion * *thatfullyveststhewarrant.CumulativeAmazonpurchasestodateare * *notdisclosedasanexplicitrunningtotal * *—the 71M is ◆ our sum of the two disclosed period figures, and the company has not stated a cumulative tally.
  • ★ The actual contra-revenue (warrant amortization) hitting the P&L is immaterial: the company states "the impact of these arrangements on the Company's consolidated financial statements was not material for the periods presented," and the warrant asset only moved ~16.0M→ 16.5M. Not disclosed as a discrete contra-revenue line; it is buried in net revenue.

Bottom line on the warrant: Amazon has started buying (≈33Min2025, ≈38M in Q1-2026, accelerating), but cumulative purchases (~$71M ◆) are <2% of the $4B trigger, and zero performance shares beyond the initial 1,324,233 have vested as of 3/31/2026. The Amazon relationship is real but still nascent; the headline 7.9M-share / $4B figure is almost entirely unearned optionality, not realized commitment.

C.3 Management guidance on data-center / AI demand

  • ★ FY2025 10-K: "Based on customer forecasts and order backlog we believe that this elevated data center demand will likely continue into 2026. We also believe that sales in our CATV market will increase in 2026." Plus the 800G-overtakes-400G "later in 2026" call (§A.1).
  • ★ Q1-2026 10-Q: demand trends "expected to continue for the foreseeable future" — but flags tariff / trade-policy / geopolitical risk that "may adversely affect customer demand, including demand from hyperscale data center customers, and could result in delays or reductions in customer orders" (new risk language vs the 10-K).
  • ◇ / Not disclosed: no explicit forward revenue dollar guidance appears in the 10-K/10-Q (quarterly guidance lives in the furnished earnings-release exhibits, which are not cached here).

C.4 Caveat / quality of the data-center "AI" story

  • ◆ Despite the AI narrative, 2025's growth engine was CATV/Digicomm, not hyperscaler optics. Data-center revenue grew a respectable +31.7% in FY2025 but its share fell; the explosive line was CATV. Only in Q1-2026 did data center re-accelerate hard (+154% YoY). The single biggest balance-sheet risk remains Digicomm ($222.7M of $299.0M AR = 74.5% at 3/31/2026), a CATV distributor — not the AI/hyperscaler story the multiple implies. ★
