1.4 Payment terms — Digicomm IS on EXTENDED terms (confirmed ★)

  • ★ Q1-2026 10-Q Note 9 (Customer Concentration), verbatim: "Management has extended longer than typical payment terms to Digicomm in order to ensure Digicomm has adequate inventory on hand to quickly provide products to customers when needed for their network builds. The Company has a multi-year history with Digicomm and over this period the Company's collection experience has been good. Digicomm's payment history has generally been relatively consistent over time… Management expects Accounts Receivable concentration with Digicomm to decline" as data-center customers grow. "The Company… does not believe this concentration will create a material credit-risk."
  • The specific net-X day count (net 30/60/90) for Digicomm or any customer is Not disclosed numerically — the filing only characterizes them qualitatively as "longer than typical." The company "generally does not require deposits or collateral to support customer receivables" ★ (FY2025 10-K Note 8).

1.5 DSO (days sales outstanding) — ◆ computed

◆ Q1-2026 DSO = AR ÷ quarterly revenue × 91 = $298.996M ÷ $151.144M × 91 = 180.0 days.

Decomposition (◆, using Digicomm sub-figures):

  • ◆ Digicomm-only DSO = $222.7M ÷ $66.7M × 91 = ≈ 304 days (AR is ~3.3× one quarter of Digicomm revenue — consistent with the "extended terms" admission).
  • ◆ Non-Digicomm DSO = ($299.0M − 222.7M) ÷ (151.144M − $66.7M) × 91 = $76.3M ÷ $84.4M × 91 = ≈ 82 days (normal-ish).
  • ◆ FY2025 annual DSO = $244.404M ÷ $455.715M × 365 = ≈ 196 days; ◆ FY2024 = $116.801M ÷ $249.365M × 365 = ≈ 171 days.

Takeaway: the blended 180-day Q1-2026 DSO is almost entirely a Digicomm artifact — receivables from Digicomm equate to roughly three quarters of sales to that customer, while the rest of the book turns in ~82 days. This is a vendor-financing-like arrangement (AAOI funds Digicomm's channel inventory) booked as ordinary trade AR.

2.2 When depreciation starts — CIP is NOT yet depreciating

  • ★ FY2025 10-K (PP&E accounting policy): "Construction in progress represents property, plant and equipment under construction or being installed… Construction in progress is transferred to the appropriate fixed asset account and depreciation commences when the asset has been substantially completed and placed in service."
  • Therefore the $39.8M (FY2025) / $51.5M (Q1-2026) of CIP carries zero accumulated depreciation and is NOT yet hitting the P&L. ◆ CIP = 6.5% of gross PP&E at FY2025 (39.808 / (568.054 + 39.808 + 1.101)). As this CIP is placed in service, depreciation expense will step up — a forward drag on gross margin not yet reflected in current D&A.
  • ★ No capitalized interest in FY2025/FY2024/FY2023 ("there was no capitalized interest").

2.4 Capacity utilization / fixed-overhead absorption commentary

  • ★ FY2025 10-K (Risk Factors): "We have a high fixed cost base due to our vertically integrated business model, including the fact that 4,116 of our employees as of December 31, 2025 were employed in manufacturing and R&D… Our gross profit and gross margin are greatly affected by our sales volume and volatility on a quarterly basis and the corresponding absorption of fixed manufacturing overhead expenses. … insufficient demand… may subject us to the risk of high inventory carrying costs and increased inventory obsolescence."
  • ★ FY2025 gross-margin MD&A (the positive absorption story): the improvement in gross margin FY2024→FY2025 "was primarily driven by larger scale production of our CATV products, which resulted in improved manufacturing efficiencies and lower cost variances." → operating leverage / better fixed-overhead absorption as CATV volume scaled.
  • ★ Margin trajectory (income statement): gross margin 24.8% (FY2024) → 30.0% (FY2025); COGS as % of revenue 75.2% → 70.0%. Q1-2026 gross margin 29.1% vs Q1-2025 30.6% (slight sequential give-back; COGS 70.9% vs 69.4%).
  • ★ COGS bridge FY2024→FY2025: "+$131.2M, or 70.0%… primarily attributable to higher direct material and direct labor costs associated with increased revenue volumes, as well as an increase in inventory reserves… consistent with the higher inventory levels and overall business expansion."
  • No explicit numeric "% capacity utilization" metric is disclosed. "Not disclosed" for a quantified utilization rate; commentary is qualitative (absorption tied to volume).
